HEIR Fragrances: Priyanka Malhotra’s ₹2 Crore Bet on Indian Luxury Perfumery

July 7, 2026

HEIR launched in July 2026 with five original Eau de Parfums, a perfumer who worked at Jo Malone and Tom Ford, and ₹2 crore in pre-seed from a roster of Indian startup founders.

The brand positions itself as India’s first globally respected luxury fragrance house, targeting the shelf next to Creed, Byredo, and Diptyque.

The People Behind HEIR

Founder: Priyanka Malhotra.

The perfumer: Michael Ælfric Nordstrand. His credits include fragrance development for Jo Malone and Tom Ford. HEIR invested 18 months of development with him before releasing a single bottle.

‍ The Development Timeline

In an industry where clone houses move from brief to shelf in weeks, HEIR spent 18 months developing five compositions with a single perfumer. That timeline tells you more about the ambition than any press release.

The backers: HEIR closed a ₹2 crore pre-seed round from founders who built real Indian consumer brands.

Niraj Singh (Spinny), Sangeet Agrawal and Navin Parwal (Mokobara), Arjun Vaidya (V3 Ventures), Kishore Biyani (Future Group), Bala Sarda (Vahdam), Deep Bajaj (Sirona Hygiene), Suyash Saraf (Dot and Key), and Radhika Ghai (Kindlife).

These are operators who scaled D2C brands in India. They know the distribution challenges, the pricing psychology, and the customer acquisition costs. HEIR is building for the long term with this roster.

The Target Audience

The brand targets a specific buyer: someone who owns Creed or Byredo and wants an Indian alternative at the same quality level. Each fragrance is blended in Spain using globally sourced raw materials. The bottles are custom ribbed glass with velvet-lined presentation boxes.

The Five Fragrances

HEIR debuted with five distinct compositions, all original. The perfumer created them from scratch rather than following a brief to clone or improve on an existing hit.

The five compositions span distinct territories. Atlas goes for Middle Eastern codes with saffron and oud. Miras uses cardamom and malted cacao in a spicy gourmand direction. Kedara works meditative Japanese cedar and hinoki. The collection reads as a perfumer’s showcase.

HEIR describes its philosophy around “traces”: the idea that fragrance carries history and memory across generations, and that an heir chooses what to carry forward and what to reimagine.

The Bigger Picture

The timing connects to a broader shift. Amazon Beautyverse 2026 put Middle Eastern fragrances in the spotlight as India’s biggest beauty trend just weeks before HEIR launched. The Indian buyer is more fragrance conscious than a year ago. A homegrown luxury house entering now is responding to demand that already exists.

India has strong attar traditions and a handful of indie perfume brands. None have positioned themselves as a global luxury house from day one. HEIR wants to sit next to Creed and Tom Ford. That requires a different approach to ingredients, packaging, perfumer relationships, and retail strategy.

‍ The Indian Luxury Gap

India produces some of the world’s finest raw fragrance ingredients, including Mysore sandalwood, rose from Kannauj, and ruh khus from North India. Yet no Indian brand has built a global luxury perfume house. HEIR is the first to try with this level of backing and perfumer pedigree.

The founders put ₹2 crore behind this. The perfumer spent 18 months developing five compositions. The packaging is custom. This is a brand built for years.

HEIR plans to expand internationally by 2027 with new fragrances and objects for the house.
