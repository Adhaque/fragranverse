🍊 Middle East — The Community's Favourite

The most-discussed tier this month. Middle Eastern houses dominate because they deliver designer-level quality at clone prices. Here are the three getting the most attention right now.

Hawas Ice takes the Invictus Aqua DNA and turns the temperature down. That frozen apple note in the opening is sharp, almost minty—it cuts through Indian humidity better than the original. The mid brings a sweet-plum warmth that keeps it from being one-dimensional, while the driftwood-and-ambergris base holds for a solid 8–10 hours. On skin, the projection is aggressive for the first two hours before settling into a comfortable skin scent. This is not a sit-down fragrance—it announces itself.

Tagged with #Beast_Mode, #Summer, #Humidity_Proof, and #Monsoon in the FragranVerse Database—meaning it's one of only 39 perfumes marked as humidity-resistant.

🏆 Best for
Indian summers and high-humidity days when you want a freshie that won't disappear in 20 minutes. Ideal for outdoor daytime wear.
Skip if
You prefer subtle, office-safe scents. Hawas Ice projects hard and stays loud.

Kaaf isn't a direct clone of any single perfume—it's an original composition from Ahmed Al Maghribi that competes in the same fresh-aquatic space as Hawas and Invictus. The opening is unmistakable: a juicy watermelon accented with lavender and Sicilian orange that smells like summer in a bottle. It's sweeter and more floral than Hawas Ice, with a lily-of-the-valley and jasmine heart that leans unisex. The sandalwood-musk base keeps it grounded without getting heavy.

Tagged #Summer, #Daily_Wear, and #Office_Safe in the DB—making it the more versatile choice between the two. It's the one you grab when you want to smell good without clearing the room.

🏆 Best for
Everyday office wear and casual summer outings. Safe blind buy if you like freshies.
Skip if
You want beast-mode projection. Kaaf sits closer to the skin after the first hour.

Theoreme does what few clones manage: it captures the spirit of Bvlgari Tygar without smelling like a cheap knock-off. The grapefruit-ginger opening is bright, effervescent, and feels expensive. The aquatic-cedar mid bridges into a vetiver-musk base that lasts through a full workday. Where Turathi Blue (also a Tygar-inspired scent) pushes harder with Invictus elements, Theoreme stays truer to the original's minimalist DNA.

Tagged #Daily_Wear, #Daytime, #Summer, #Beach, and #Monsoon—it's built for the kind of heat that makes heavy scents cloying. If you're after one bottle that works from the gym to dinner, this is it.

🏆 Best for
Anyone who wants a sophisticated Tygar-like scent without spending ₹15,000+. Perfect for hot climates.
Skip if
You want strong projection. Theoreme sits at moderate and doesn't push for compliments.

Turathi Blue takes the same Bvlgari Tygar inspiration as Theoreme but pushes it in a different direction—toward Invictus territory. The pepper-and-mandarin opening is sharper, more synthetic in a good way, and the aquatic-lavender heart gives it that blue-freshie punch. It projects noticeably harder than Theoreme and lasts longer on skin, making it the choice for anyone who wants their presence known.

Tagged #Beast_Mode, #Summer, #All_Season, and #Monsoon in the DB. At a community street price of around ₹1,800–₹2,200 for 100ml, it's one of the strongest value propositions in this entire list.

⚠ Hot Climate Note
Turathi Blue's Invictus-style aquatic sweetness can become cloying above 38°C. Consider Theoreme if you live in a city that hits 42°C regularly.
🏆 Best for
High-performance summer wear. If you want beast-mode projection in a fresh profile, this is your pick.
Skip if
You prefer subtlety. Turathi Blue announces itself from across the room.

Bright Peach by Maison Alhambra goes head-to-head with Tom Ford's Bitter Peach (₹17,000+) and comes surprisingly close. The peach here is ripe and syrupy rather than bitter—backed by cardamom and green leaves that give it a fresh opening. The mid brings rum and patchouli for depth, and the honey-vanilla-tonka base is warm without getting cloying. It's sweeter and more approachable than the Tom Ford original, which works in its favour for the Indian climate.

Tagged #Fall and #Spring in the DB—meaning it's not a scorching-summer scent, but it handles moderate heat well. The community street price of around ₹1,500–₹1,800 makes it one of the most affordable ways to experience the Bitter Peach profile.

🏆 Best for
Fans of sweet, boozy-fruity scents who want a Tom Ford alternative without the price tag.

FAQ

Is Hawas Ice a clone?
Yes, Hawas Ice by Rasasi is inspired by Invictus Aqua by Paco Rabanne. It's a Middle Eastern clone with a frozen-apple twist that gives it a cooler, mintier profile than the original.

Is Kaaf by Ahmed Al Maghribi a clone?
Kaaf is an original composition, not a direct clone. Its fresh watermelon-lavender profile competes in the same category as Invictus and Hawas Ice but brings its own floral-fruity character.

What is Theoreme a clone of?
Theoreme by Rue Broca is inspired by Bvlgari Tygar. It delivers a grapefruit-ginger-citrus profile with moderate sillage and long-lasting performance at a fraction of Tygar's price.
