Rasasi Dropped 3 New Hawas in 2026 — Thunder, Exotic & Lava Gold. Which One's Worth Your Money?

June 27, 2026 · 6 min read · New Releases

Rasasi's Hawas line is built on a simple promise: niche-adjacent performance at budget prices. The original Hawas for Him became the ₹2,000 benchmark for Indian buyers by delivering what designer houses charge 5× more for. The three 2026 flankers — Thunder, Exotic, and Lava Gold — follow that same philosophy. They're inspired-by fragrances, each targeting a different mood, season, and personality. Here's the DB-verified breakdown.

Flanker Family Longevity Sillage Best For
Hawas Thunder Woody 6–8 hrs Moderate Office / daytime
Hawas Exotic Oriental 6–8 hrs Strong Evening / date night
Hawas Lava Gold Spicy 10+ hrs Heavy Night out / winter

All three are already in the FragranVerse database with verified notes, performance ratings, and olfactory family classifications. Let's go through each one.

Thunder is Rasasi playing it smart. It opens with bergamot, dried apple, and mandarin — a citrus-spice combination that immediately reads familiar and inoffensive. The ginger gives it a slight bite that keeps it from being boring. The woody drydown is clean and unobtrusive. Think of it as Hawas Ice's bolder cousin: same approachability, more spice and projection.

In Indian weather, Thunder is the most versatile of the three. It won't suffocate in March heat and won't disappear in December cool. That makes it the safest buy if you're picking just one from the new trio.

🏆 Best for
Office wear, metro commutes, neutral social settings. Anyone who liked Hawas Ice but wanted more projection and a hint of spice.
Skip if
You already own Hawas Ice and want something completely different. Thunder is family, not a stranger.

Exotic is getting the least early attention of the three — Thunder has the "safe buy" crowd, Lava Gold has the beast-mode crowd, and Exotic is sitting between them. That's what makes it interesting. The DB shows it has the most layered note profile of the 2026 trio: Oriental family with warm florals and a strong sillage that fills a room without going nuclear.

The critical context for Indian buyers: Oriental fragrances in Indian summers can be challenging. Exotic is firmly an evening fragrance. Try it after 7 PM first. In AC environments it'll behave beautifully — in 38°C outdoor heat it'll be more aggressive than you'd want.

💡 Best for
Date nights, formal dinners, festive occasions. The person who finds Thunder too fresh and Lava Gold too heavy — Exotic is the middle ground with genuine character.
Skip if
Oriental florals aren't your thing, or you're buying for daytime wear in Indian summer heat.

The FragranVerse DB rates Lava Gold at Eternal longevity (10+ hrs) and Heavy sillage — the highest tier in both categories. That puts it in genuine beast-mode territory. The spicy opening transitions into a smoky incense heart with a dark resinous base that projects and lingers for hours. Early reviews from the Indian fragrance community on Reddit specifically call it a cold-weather weapon that fills a room the moment you walk in.

For Indian collectors: if you live in North India where winters actually get cold (Delhi, Punjab, UP), Lava Gold is the buy of the three. It's the fragrance that justifies wearing a heavy hitter. If you're in Mumbai or Chennai where "winter" means 25°C — Thunder or Exotic will serve you better year-round.

⚠️ Indian summer warning

Heavy + Eternal longevity in 38°C heat = overwhelming. Lava Gold is a cold-weather and evening fragrance. Don't try it in summer daytime — you'll hate it and the experience won't represent what it actually is.

🏆 Best for
Night outs, winter evenings, anyone who wants the nuclear performer in the Hawas lineup. North India winter buyers: this is a strong yes.
Skip if
You live somewhere with 8+ months of summer, or you're new to heavy oriental fragrances. Start with Thunder first, build up to Lava Gold.

The Original vs the Trio — Where Does Hawas for Him Sit Now?

The 2026 trio doesn't replace the original Hawas for Him — it extends the line into different moods. The original is still the most versatile entry point: aquatic-woody, great projection, all-year wearability, sub-₹2,000. If you don't own the original, start there before branching into the flankers.

The Bottom Line

Rasasi's 2026 Hawas trio shows the brand is deliberately diversifying its best-selling DNA rather than just releasing watered-down cash grabs. Thunder is the safe crowd-pleaser, Exotic is the underrated character pick, and Lava Gold is the genuine beast for collectors who know exactly what they want.
