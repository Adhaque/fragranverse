Concentration — EDP vs EDT vs Parfum, explained

Concentration is the percentage of perfume oil in the bottle — more oil means a stronger scent, longer longevity, and a higher price. Parfum (Extrait) runs 20–40% oil and lasts 8+ hours; EDP (Eau de Parfum) sits at 15–20% and lasts 5–8 hours; EDT (Eau de Toilette) is 5–15% and lasts 3–5 hours. For Indian summers, an EDT or a fresh EDP works best — heavy Parfums can choke in humidity. Always check the concentration before buying, and never compare performance across different concentrations.

Ambroxan — the clean, salty wood everyone wears

Ambroxan is a synthetic that mimics ambergris: a dry, slightly salty, woody-musky warmth that hugs the skin and radiates without shouting. It's the backbone of modern "fresh but expensive" scents and behaves brilliantly in Indian heat — it doesn't turn sour like sweeter notes can. If a fragrance smells "clean but addictive," ambroxan is usually why.

Iso E Super — the invisible amplifier

Iso E Super is a velvety, cedar-like molecule that's barely noticeable up close but radiates beautifully in your trail. It creates the effortless "your skin but better" effect — the secret behind Molecule 01 and Not a Perfume. Layer a drop under a bold floral or woody scent to add depth and projection without competing with the main notes.
